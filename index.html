Person

I am a full­stack developer with 3+ years of professional ex­perience, and a focus on back­end develop­ment in Python. I have an ana­lytical mind­set and I am of a communi­cative and social nature. My main drivers are curio­sity and self-efficacy.

Analytical Mindset

I have a sharp and ready mind. I am able to identify logical vagueness or con­tra­dictions easily and can ask the right ques­tions to re­solve them. I have streng­thened this skill during studying philo­sophy and in uni­versity de­bating. As a soft­ware de­veloper, I am able to use this skill for instance in re­finements with the stake­holders.

Communication

I am an open, communi­cative and relatable person. I like arguments that are both rational and respect­ful. I love teaching and men­toring. In university, I have been a tutor for philo­sophy and logics. I have also spent a lot of time in de­bating.

In the job, this communicative side of mine appears in that I like to contribute to dis­cussions about soft­ware architecture decisions or certain programming solutions with peers, that I love men­toring juniors and that I also like communi­cating with non-tech colleagues, for instance from the product side.

I have taught pro­gramming in Python to a former non-tech colleague. I have also given cour­ses about SQL, Re­gular Ex­pressions and git to non-tech colleagues in different companies.

My ex­perience in de­bating and public speaking has taught me not to be afraid to present ideas to an audience. In the job as a software developer, I use this skill when pre­senting arguments on rather complex or abstract topics, such as re­factoring or soft­ware design decisions.

Social

In persons, I value self-reflection, kind­ness and modesty, and I try to live by these values myself. I identify with the group I am working with, and try to find the best so­lutions for the team. I value coöperation over com­petition and I find pleasure in meaning­ful social inter­action. I en­courage people to critizise me and I see critique or feedback as a valuable re­source for im­provement. I de­liver con­struc­tive feed­back in a pro­fessional and em­pathic manner which takes the other person's per­spective in­to account.

Curiosity

I am curious and con­sider my journey as a soft­ware developer as a lifelong-learning pro­ject. Cur­rently I am most in­terested in soft­ware archi­tecture and in strengthening my Dev­Ops and front­end skills.

Self-Efficacy

Usually, when pre­sented with any status quo, I have a lot of ideas of what to do. I find pleasure in chal­lenging and im­proving the status quo. Aside my work, I have realized some side-projects and I have tons of project stubs in my personal back­log.
