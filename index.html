Person

I am a fullstack developer with 3+ years of professional experience, and a focus on backend development in Python. I have an analytical mindset and I am of a communicative and social nature. My main drivers are curiosity and self-efficacy.

Analytical Mindset

I have a sharp and ready mind. I am able to identify logical vagueness or contradictions easily and can ask the right questions to resolve them. I have strengthened this skill during studying philosophy and in university debating. As a software developer, I am able to use this skill in refinements with the stakeholders.

Communication

I am an open, communicative and relatable person. I like arguments that are both rational and respectful. I love teaching and mentoring. In university, I have been a tutor for philosophy and logics. I have also spent a lot of time in debating.

In the job, this communicative side of me appears in that I like discussing software architecture decisions or certain programming solutions with peers, mentoring juniors and also communicating with non-tech colleagues, for instance from the product side.

Later, as a developer, I have taught programming in Python to a former non-tech colleague. I have also given courses about SQL, Regular Expressions and git to non-tech colleagues in different companies.

My experience in debating and public speaking has taught me not to be afraid to present ideas to an audience. In the job as a software developer, I use this skill when presenting arguments on rather complex or abstract topics, such as refactoring or software design decisions.

Social

In persons, I value self-reflection, kindness and modesty, and I try to live by these values myself. I identify with the group I am working with, and try to find the best solutions for the team. I value cooperation over competition and I find pleasure in meaningful social interaction. I encourage people to give me to critizise me and I see critique or feedback as a valuable resource for improvement. I delover constructive feedback in a professional and empathic way which takes the other person's perspective into account.

Curiosity

I am curious and consider my journey as a software developer as a lifelong-learning project. Currently I am most interested in software architecture strenghtening my DevOps and Frontend skills.

Self-Efficacy

Usually, when presented with any status quo, I have a lot of ideas of what to do. I find pleasure in challenging and improving the status quo. Aside my work, I have realized some side-projects and I have tons of project stubs in my personal backlog.
